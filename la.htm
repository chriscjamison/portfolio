Leaders Academy

Fall 2017

Viewing Info

Site optimized for mobile browsers. View Leaders Academy

The development cycle of a mobile-only website for Leaders Academy presented opportunities to build resourcefulness and ingenuity – as a programmer and communicator.

Leaders Academy is an English language debate school based in Seoul, South Korea. The school teaches young, middle-school aged boys and girls debate skills to raise their academic readiness.

Leaders Academy sought a mobile-friendly website to compliment their desktop web experience. The mobile site ordered by Leaders Academy was intended to extend the reach of their web presence by allowing parents and teachers to access pertinent and recent news and content about the school. Potential members of the school could view marketing materials and a map for directions to the school’s two campuses.

After the management at Leaders Academy presented photos of paper mockups, digital mockups were produced.

But there was a problem with the delivered mockups.

On the paper mockups, the content sections were defined in a set order. . Only I didn’t know the sections were drawn in a pre-determined order. As a designer, the paper mockups were a starting point to casually rearrange until a more refined UI was developed.

The stakeholders however invested thought and consideration to arrive at that exact configuration of the layout. They voiced frustration with the unwanted changes.

Stunned by the significance of lapse of judgement, I apologized to the stakeholders. But the moment increased my respect for their business knowledge.

The gaffe taught a lesson. Clients may not have design but they know good design when they see it. Their opinion is the ultimate one.

After wiping the egg whites away from my face, which of course took place after I set my shoe down from my mouth, I updated the digital mockups.

After a few more rounds of revisions, we reached agreement on the final UI.

The front-end development begun, which turned out to be more complex than I originally envisioned.

The HTML and Javascript needed to render the actual UI was relatively straight-forward. Complications became evident when meshing the CMS of the desktop site to serve content to the mobile site.

The CMS for their desktop site was message board software repurposed to serve typical web content such as copy, images, and the like. The software did not have the default functionality to serve content into a mobile only template.

Since the expected time of launch was soon approaching and technical support from the software company was not available, it was necessary to conceive a quick-to-implement workaround.

It became clear quickly that there wasn’t enough time to fully create a solution which would pull content from the desktop site and inject it into the mobile template. But there was enough time to code a static site.

The site was ready for the initial launch date which coincided with an open house for prospective parents.

I then began to develop a scheme to load the content housed in the desktop CMS into the mobile experience.

The taken approach required a jQuery script which first extracted the content from a loading webpage. Then the jQuery script replaced the HTML from the desktop site which housed the content with mobile-friendly HTML. Lastly, the content was then inserted into the freshly loaded mobile template.

The completed mobile-friendly website better suited the initial goals of Leaders Academy for their website by serving the latest content to the mobile template without requiring hand-coding pages for fresh content.

 
 

Takeaways

Experiencing the displeasure from the stakeholders at Leaders Academy after they reviewed my initial mockups was a good growth experience.

The sharp feedback from the client was a surprising but led to a valuable insight.

The stakeholder knew their business in a way and to a depth which I could never know. And as a result the mockup they presented reflected their high level of sensitivity to their customer’s needs.
