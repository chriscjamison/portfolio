Las Vegas Pro Soccer 2018

Summer 2017

Web Address

Launch version hosted on personal server http://chriscjamison.com/lvps/

Working with Las Vegas Pro Soccer 2018 (LVPS) afforded an opportunity to learn and utilize business communications skills with a decision-maker, a fellow web developer, and content producers.

Las Vegas Pro Soccer 2018 was an organization tasked with marketing the arrival of a United Soccer League expansion team in Las Vegas, NV for the 2018 regular season. The organization solicited a website to aid the collection of deposits for season tickets, add future fans of the team to an email contact list, and provide snippets of content intended to answer questions related to this new team.

Developing this website invited a variety of challenges. Chiefly, the condensed timeline of the development cycle. The decision-maker at LVPS required the completion of the site to take place within two weeks.

Usually, the development cycles of previous projects required one month minimum. A majority of projects have taken at least three months worktime.

LVPS’s website wasn’t the first website I have worked on as a freelancer – previous projects included websites made for family members and personal friends. But working with LVPS was the first time working for a professional business organization. A working website, delivered on-time, would be vital to the success of the organization

The decision-maker and I had a prior relationship – we attended the same university and were dorm friends. I even remember hanging out in his apartment watching professional soccer games. Talk about living out a dream.

However, we hadn’t worked together before.

While my time as a freelancer has been short, it’s become clear that in personal relationships and business relationships, honesty is important, if not the catalyst which powers productive and respectful communication.

Resolving the conflict required focus and a willingness to be transparent about a misjudgement.

The client asked if this site would be easily viewable in mobile devices. Because of the limited space for content included in the website template the site for LVPS was built upon and the limited amount of time for development, completing a mobile-friendly website was impossible.

I didn’t want to disappoint my client, but I knew promising an outcome which was reasonably unlikely to happen would only increase his dissatisfaction. I told him directly but mindfully that I couldn’t create the mobile-friendly site.

However, the honesty was rewarded because due to the brilliance of a fellow web developer, brought on mid-project, a mobile-friendly site was quickly produced and available at the time of launch.

Whew!

Thanks to the project management of by the decision-maker, the dedication of my fellow web developer and content producers working for LVPS, the site was completed on time. Only an hour ahead of time, but still on time.

 
 

Takeaways

To reach the final version of the website you can view, it required a number of revisions, some made in the last days of the project. The revisions made for a better product. I used to take revisions as a dismissal of my skill but with each round of revisions, the UI and UX became noticeably better. Since then, I’ve tried to include more takes during my initial submission of designs.

Collaboration was easier than it initially seemed. It helped that everyone involved with the project was very professional and capable. Like any relationship, it takes two and having good partners made communication productive and timely.

I was fortunate to have such a good client. His management style encouraged me to do my best work and feel comfortable sharing obstacles which caused complications.

Working along side a more experienced and knowledgeable developer increased my awareness of what I didn’t know. But that new awareness pointed me to learn new technologies such as Bootstrap 4. And also, it pointed out that it is possible to perform high-quality work without having an expert level of knowledge of UI design and front-end development.
