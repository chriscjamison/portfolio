Working as a freelance UI/UX designer and front-end developer adds depth to my design and development process.

My process began a long time ago in Missouri during my college years.

A friend of mine was running for Student Government President. At that time the Web began to become a popular medium. At least more than only Computer Science students surfing around to distract their minds from completing programming assignments.

I don’t remember who — my friend, another one of his campaign workers, or myself — had the brilliant thought that a website would help the campaign isn’t clear. But once my friend, the candidate, decided the website was a good idea, it was up to someone to design the site.

Seeing that my friend was a Political Science major and didn’t care much for technology, it wasn’t going to be him.

I stepped in. About a week later, a working website was complete.

Eventually, the election came around and my friend’s campaign won. It turned out the website’s content swayed voters his way.

Creating a website was so simple then. Reflecting back on the early days of the web, the fundamentals of web design are the same but oh so more complex.

My current process is evolving but includes many contemporary approaches.

Robust exploration of the visual design elements and UX guidelines underpinning the layout of high-res mockups with greater visual impact and an improves user experience. Mockups took less time because the ‘joyous’ tedium of making design decisions was complete.

Recent self-paced education on modern design fundamentals such as responsive design and semantic markup within HTML5 revealed new techniques to incorporate.

UI’s were conceived to display content in browsers as small as an iPhone and up to 1080p displays. Details, such as navigation – laid out to suit mobile devices – and larger images and font sizes for 1080p displays are nods to responsive design theories.

Professional touches such as detailed commenting of HTML and JS and text descriptions of the content of layers within wireframes are common aspects of my workflow.

Using easy to identify variable names and simple yet descriptive comments to highlight the function of code make following after my work easier for fellow designers and developers.

Communicating with clients about their wishes for the website used by their organization shed light on the many intricacies within client/business relationships. A growing business empathy feeds curiosity about the needs of clients which adds nuance to the delivered product.

Thank you for reading this description of my recent professional background.

I look forward to share in more detail the ways my skills and approaches can help your organization reach its goals.

Please contact me via phone, email, or LinkedIn when you have time.
